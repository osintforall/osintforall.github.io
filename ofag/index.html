Welcome to Osint For All Guild

OFA Guild is a hierarchical order to define and self analyze your OSINT caliber. It is distinguished into three main categories - Apprentice, Journey Person and Master. You could be in any one of those categories at a given point of time, so you are free to put yourself in whatever the category you wish to. In order to get any badge, join our Discord Server, subscribe to our YouTube channel(also press the bell icon), follow us on Twitter and solve the challenges that we post occasionally.

Apprentice

An apprentice is a person who could or could not be a novice Internet user. If you are using the Internet for a long time and you don’t know how it works or if you are a person who has just started using the Internet through the means of social media (Facebook, Twitter, Snapchat, Instagram etc), you are at the right place and there is no better time to learn about the Internet. All learning is possible by “Googling”. More you learn, the more you become aware.

  • History of the Internet
  • What is the Web?
  • What are Browsers?
  • What are Search Engines?
  • What is Social Media?
  • Why am I getting the Internet for free?
  • Why am I getting to use social media for free of cost?
  • Where am I getting all this information on the Internet? Who is providing me all this information?
  • Where is all my data going?
  • Why am I using the Internet in the first place?
  • What’s the use of learning OSINT?
  • How can I get basic information on the Internet? (Googling, Maps, Wayback Machine)
  • And always share what you have learnt if you want or if you can without any hesitation

Journeyperson

A journeyperson is the one who has to have a clear vision about why they want to do OSINT, what’s their purpose or rationale or agenda behind learning and becoming a OSINTer. If you are someone who wants to pursue a career in OSINT, you are at the right place. Learn about the following things.

  • Applications of OSINT
  • Passive Recon
  • Active Recon
  • What’s my goal in life? What are my values?
  • On a technical level, how comfortable am I with the Internet and computers in general?
  • What’s MEDIA? (Digital, Mainstream, Independent)
  • What’s cyber security? How relevant is OSINT in cyber security?
  • How can I create my own “system” to find sources, gather information and use that information?
  • What’s the importance of privacy and operational security in OSINT? And why should I/should I not value them?
  • What are the things that I can do to improve myself with regards to “information gathering”? (Blogs, Books, Movies, CTFs, Videos, Thinking out of the box)
  • And always share what you have learnt if you want or if you can without any hesitation

Master

A master is the one who knows that they aren’t the master in the fast changing world, even if they know, they don’t know that they know and is willing to be a lifelong learner, teaching others if they can, learning from others if they want, and not patronizing or prejudicing anyone.

  • Solve 5 cases from our Unsolvable CTF
  • Keep an eye on all things that are related to “INT”
  • Always be curious and try to invent your own “systems” or “methods” to collect and collate information
  • And always share what you have learnt if you want or if you can without any hesitation
